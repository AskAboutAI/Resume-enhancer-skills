# Offer Negotiation Reference

Covers: market salary analysis, counter-offer email, phone call script,
and walk-away decision framework. Run all four sections in sequence.

---

## The One Rule Before Anything Else

**Never give a number first.**

If the company asks "What are your salary expectations?" before making an
offer, give this response:
> "I'd like to learn more about the full scope of the role and the
> compensation package before discussing specific numbers. What is the
> budgeted range for this position?"

This deflects without damaging the relationship. It also surfaces their
ceiling before you anchor below it. Coach the user on this before building
any scripts.

## Phase 1: Market Analysis

Run tool calls first (see SKILL.md Tool Usage), then produce:

```
MARKET ANALYSIS: [ROLE] at [COMPANY] in [LOCATION]
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

THE OFFER
Base:       [Amount]
Bonus:      [Amount / % / "Not disclosed"]
Equity:     [RSUs / Options / "None"]
Benefits:   [Key items — health, PTO, remote policy]
Total comp: [Base + expected bonus, approx]

MARKET DATA
Source 1 (Indeed):    [Role] at [Company] in [Location] — [range]
Source 2 (web):       [Role] market median [Location] 2025 — [range]
Source 3 (web):       [Role] at [similar company] — [range if available]

WHERE THIS OFFER FALLS
[Below market / At market / Above market] by approximately [X%]

Reasoning:
[2-3 sentences explaining the position. Be specific about what data points
support the conclusion. If data is limited, say so — do not fabricate a
precise number from thin data.]

RECOMMENDED COUNTER-OFFER
Counter base:   [Specific number — not a range]
Reasoning:      [Why this number specifically. What data supports it.
                What it represents as a % above offer.]
Acceptable min: [The lowest base to accept if they push back]
Walk-away:      [The number below which you decline]

Note: Counter should typically be 10-20% above the offer, giving room
to land at 5-10% above without the company feeling they lost. Calibrate
to how far below market the offer is and how much the user wants this role.
```

## Phase 2: Counter-Offer Email Script

Write the exact email the user should send. It must follow all 4 rules:

1. Express genuine excitement about the role — one sentence, specific.
   Not "I am thrilled about this opportunity." Something that proves they
   actually want this role, not any role.
2. Reference market data — ground the counter in data, not in personal need.
   "Based on market data" positions it as a business discussion, not a
   negotiation.
3. State a specific number — not a range. A range signals the floor.
4. Keep it under 150 words — long emails create friction.

Apply ALL shared writing rules from SKILL.md. Especially: no em dashes,
no en dashes.

Format:
```
COUNTER-OFFER EMAIL
Subject: Re: Offer for [Role] at [Company]

[Body — under 150 words, 3 short paragraphs]

Paragraph 1: Genuine, specific excitement about the role and company.
Paragraph 2: Reference market data, state the counter number clearly.
Paragraph 3: Reaffirm interest, invite a conversation to align.

[User's name]
```

## Phase 3: Phone Call Script

Give exact language for four scenarios. These are scripts, not talking points.
The user should be able to read these verbatim in the call if needed.

Apply all shared writing rules. No em dashes, no en dashes. Spoken language
can use natural pauses but no filler — write it as clean, confident speech.

```
PHONE NEGOTIATION SCRIPT
════════════════════════════════════════════

OPENING (when they call to discuss the offer):
"Thanks for calling. I want to start by saying I'm genuinely excited about
the [Role] position and the team. This is exactly the kind of opportunity
I've been looking for. I did want to talk through the compensation to make
sure we can reach something that works for both sides."

STATING YOUR COUNTER:
"Based on my research into the market rate for this role in [Location] and
at [Company]'s level, I was expecting something closer to [counter number].
I think that reflects the value I'd bring, particularly in [specific area
from your resume that is most relevant to the role]. Would [Company] be
able to get closer to that range?"

HANDLING "THIS IS THE BEST WE CAN DO":
"I appreciate you being direct with me. Can I ask whether that applies to
the base specifically, or to the total compensation package? I want to
make sure I understand what flexibility exists before I respond."
[Then pivot to non-salary items. See the options below.]

NEGOTIATING NON-SALARY ITEMS (if base is firm):
Use these in order. Lead with the one that matters most to the user.

Signing bonus:
"Since the base has a ceiling, would there be room for a signing bonus?
Even something in the [range] range would help bridge the gap for me."

Extra PTO:
"Another option I would consider: an additional [N] days of PTO annually.
That's something I value highly and would make a real difference."

Remote flexibility:
"If base is fixed, I'd love to discuss remote flexibility. Would [X days
per week remote] be something the team could accommodate?"

Start date:
"One more option: a later start date would give me time to [wrap up
current commitments / vest equity / close a freelance project]. Would
[date] work on your end?"
```

## Phase 4: Walk-Away Analysis

Produce this framework for the user to complete with their personal numbers.
Do not invent subjective weights — give the framework and fill in the
financial math from the offer data.

```
WALK-AWAY ANALYSIS
══════════════════════════════════════════

FINANCIAL FRAMEWORK
Accept at:     [Counter accepted or close — within [X%] of counter]
Push hard at:  [Offer + 5-10% or better]
Walk away at:  [Below market median — typically the original offer if
               it's 15%+ below market]

NON-MONETARY FACTORS THAT COULD JUSTIFY A LOWER NUMBER
Score each 1-5 on importance to the user (they fill this in):

_ Career trajectory:  Does this role open doors that your current path doesn't?
_ Brand value:        Is the company name worth taking a lower number for?
_ Learning curve:     Will you grow faster here than anywhere else right now?
_ Remote flexibility: How much is not commuting worth per year to you?
_ Team quality:       Based on what you saw — is this a team you want on your resume?
_ Stability:          Is this company more or less stable than your alternatives?

DECISION RULE:
If three or more non-monetary factors score 4 or 5, a 10% salary concession
may be worth taking. If fewer than three score that high, hold the counter or
walk away. Money recovers faster than career decisions.

WHAT TO SAY IF YOU WALK AWAY:
"I've given this a lot of thought and I genuinely appreciate the offer and
the time the team spent with me. I'm not able to make it work at the current
compensation level, but I think highly of [Company] and I'd welcome staying
in touch if circumstances change on either side."

[Leave on good terms. The person who hired you will move to another company
eventually — that relationship has value beyond this offer.]
```
